TechLineages Project

Between the years of my 2nd and 3rd year, I had the opportunity to work under the supervision of Dr. Liane Gabora at UBC on her research about cultural lineages through inventions. She wanted a visual system to view how patents are related, with information relating to the inventors to give insight into potential inspirations that led to unique inventions.

The stack used a Python backend, d3 for frontend visualization, with a PostgreSQL relation database.

The visualization would scrape the Google patent API for the relevant information on the patent and store it in the database. This information would include related patents through citations, which can then be expanded when clicked on the patent. The next patents are either retrieved from the database if they exist or scraped for new information, and displayed as a web over the years displaying the relevant patent information. This was the beginning of a tool that could further be used to track new patents and learn about their inventors, as well as include AI to help filter more possible inspirations for innovations.
